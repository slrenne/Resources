# Resources

Resources for Bayesian data analysis, causal inference, epistemology, productivity, writing, lecturing, and science reading.  


## Statistical Rethinking

Richard McElreath’s Bayesian data analysis course with a strong causal-modeling emphasis. Very didactical and a good first stop for students who want intuition before formalism.

- [Course playlist / YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@rmcelreath) — McElreath’s lectures and course playlists.
- [Course materials (2025 edition)](https://github.com/rmcelreath/stat_rethinking_2025) — current course repository with slides, code, and notes.
- [Book page](https://www.routledge.com/Statistical-Rethinking-A-Bayesian-Course-with-Examples-in-R-and-STAN/McElreath/p/book/9780367139919) — the current second-edition book page.

## Bayesian data analysis

A more mathematical approach to Bayesian statistics.

- [Aki Vehtari’s course](https://avehtari.github.io/BDA_course_Aalto/gsu2023.html) — lectures with links to videos and supporting material.
- [Bayesian Data Analysis (PDF)](https://users.aalto.fi/~ave/BDA3.pdf) — the main text for the course.
- [Andrew Gelman’s book page](http://www.stat.columbia.edu/~gelman/book/) — additional material and background.

## Bayesian workflow

Tools and examples for moving from model ideas to useful analysis.

- [Aki Vehtari’s workflow video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ppKpwtGy8KQ) — a nice overview of workflow thinking.
- [Birthdays case study](https://users.aalto.fi/~ave/casestudies/Birthdays/birthdays.html) — a compact applied example.
- [McElreath’s workflow video](https://youtu.be/oHWNexYPFTY?si=DPIN-00f9kNR8wGL&t=1259) — workflow discussed from a practical modeling perspective.
- [Slides](http://talmo.uk/2024/slides/mcelreath.pdf) — companion slides for the talk above.

## Causal inference and modeling

A set of entry points from statistics and philosophy of science.

- [McElreath’s causal inference course](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KNPYUVmY3NM) — useful because parts of it overlap with the statistical rethinking material.
- [Judea Pearl, *Causality*](https://books.google.com/books/edition/Causality/f4nuexsNVZIC?hl=it&gbpv=1) — foundational work on causal reasoning.
- [Pearl keynote at PyData](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZaPV1OSEpHw) — a short and accessible talk.
- [The Causal Primer](http://bayes.cs.ucla.edu/PRIMER/) — short book with chapters available for download.
- [Jonas Peters MIT course](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zvrcyqcN9Wo) — especially useful for bivariate causal questions.
- [Jonas Peters handout](https://people.math.ethz.ch/~jopeters/jonas_files/scriptChapter1-4.pdf) — course notes.
- [Jonas Peters, *Elements of Causal Inference*](https://mitp-content-server.mit.edu/books/content/sectbyfn?collid=books_pres_0&id=11283&fn=11283.pdf) — compact and practical.
- [Jonas Peters website](https://people.math.ethz.ch/~jopeters/) — more resources and links.
- [Dagitty](http://www.dagitty.net/) — a very handy web tool for directed acyclic graphs.

## Stan

A platform for statistical modeling and computation.

- [Stan](https://mc-stan.org/) — the main project site.
- [Stan user guide](https://mc-stan.org/docs/stan-users-guide/linear-regression.html) — a practical starting point.
- [Stan YouTube page](https://www.youtube.com/@stan3394) — official talks and tutorials.
- [Bob Carpenter talk 1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6NXRCtWQNMg) — one of the better introductions from a Stan developer.
- [Bob Carpenter talk 2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qQFF4tPgeWI) — another useful talk.
- [Michael Betancourt talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uSjsJg8fcwY) — strong conceptual framing.
- [Michael Betancourt’s website](https://betanalpha.github.io/writing/) — excellent tutorials and essays.

## Data visualization

Introductory material and one good book on reading charts critically.

- [Introduction to Data Science, chapter 11](https://rafalab.dfci.harvard.edu/dsbook/data-visualization-principles.html) — a nice introduction with examples of bad graphs.
- [How Charts Lie: Getting Smarter about Visual Information](https://wwnorton.com/books/9781324001560) — practical guidance on spotting misleading charts and visual claims.
- Very nice and enjoyable [*Data Visualization. A practical introduction* by Kieran Healy](https://socviz.co).

## Epistemology

A few pieces that are helpful when talking about scientific knowledge, uncertainty, and method.

- [Science Is Like a Chicken Coop](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d8LqFO1dk-w) — a memorable McElreath talk about science and inference.
- [Science as Amateur Software Development](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zwRdO9_GGhY) — another good McElreath talk on scientific practice.
- [Science as Amateur Software Development (2023 edition)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8qzVV7eEiaI) — updated version of the same theme.
- [The Problem with Cultural Evolution](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ez3o3uWRSyY) — useful for discussions about explanation and evidence.
- [Karl Popper, *Conjectures and Refutations*](https://books.google.com/books/about/Conjectures_and_Refutations.html?id=IENmxiVBaSoC) — classic statement of the growth of scientific knowledge through conjecture and criticism.
- [Thomas S. Kuhn, *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*](https://www.google.com/books/edition/The_Structure_of_Scientific_Revolutions/) — the classic account of scientific paradigm change.
- [Paul Feyerabend, *Against Method*](https://www.versobooks.com/products/763-against-method) — a provocative critique of methodological rules in science.

## Communication

Resources for clear prose, scientific writing, and teaching writing to others.

### Writing - Talks and handouts

- [Larry McEnerney: writing effectively](https://youtu.be/vtIzMaLkCaM?si=gkgWVZliXSxcSBqF) — a strong introduction to academic writing ([transcript](files/transcript_McEnerney_writing_effectively_c.txt) of the talk and [handout](files/UnivChic_WritingProg-1grt232.pdf)).
- [Larry McEnerney: writing beyond the academy](https://youtu.be/aFwVf5a3pZM?si=c3DS8LCOe7d0OUPJ) — especially useful for writing for broader audiences ([transcript](files/transcript_McEnerney_writing_beyond_c.txt).
- [Judith Swan: scientific writing](https://youtu.be/jLPCdDp_LE0?si=gHMsedaPjB7FdYaj) — practical advice from the Princeton Writing Program.
- [Handout](files/swan_handout.pdf) — companion notes for Swan’s talk.
- [The Science of Scientific Writing](files/How_to_speak.md) — concise notes on the classic article.

### Writing - Books

- [Steven Pinker, *The Sense of Style*](https://stevenpinker.com/publications/sense-style-thinking-persons-guide-writing-21st-century) — a modern, evidence-aware guide to clear prose.
- [Francis-Noël Thomas and Mark Turner, *Clear and Simple as the Truth*](https://classicprose.com/) — a classic on prose style and transparent writing.
- [Roy Peter Clark, *Writing Tools: 50 Essential Strategies for Every Writer*](https://www.littlebrown.com/titles/roy-peter-clark/writing-tools-10th-anniversary-edition/9781549108600/) — practical sentence-level and revision tools.

## Lecturing

Advice on presenting ideas clearly and memorably.

- [Patrick Winston, *How to Speak*](https://youtu.be/Unzc731iCUY?si=93UujYCs90g6fOQ6) — a classic lecture on speaking well.
- [Notes](files/How_to_speak.md) — companion notes.
- [Chip and Dan Heath, *Made to Stick*](https://heathbrothers.com/books/made-to-stick/) — useful for designing talks that people actually remember.

## Productivity

Books and videos that are especially useful for organizing work, projects, and collaboration.

- [David Allen, *Getting Things Done*](https://gettingthingsdone.com/getting-things-done-the-art-of-stress-free-productivity/) — the core GTD book for personal productivity and stress-free execution.
- [David Allen and Edward Lamont, *Team: Getting Things Done with Others*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/717361/team-by-david-allen-and-edward-lamont/) — GTD applied to team collaboration, communication, and follow-through.

### Curated David Allen videos on YouTube

- [GTD channel](https://www.youtube.com/user/gtd) — the official GTD YouTube channel.
- [Overview of Getting Things Done (GTD)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iqTjCE-bHPM) — a compact explanation of the method.
- [How to Decide What to Do](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fl93FS6D1VQ) — a short, practical talk on next actions and choosing work.
- [Doing Nothing](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vYnncMH0bxo) — a useful reminder that not every moment needs a task.
- [David Allen and Ed Lamont talk about Team](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KTKJ2RAei7o) — a good companion to the team book.
- [The Art of Stress-Free Productivity at TEDxClaremontColleges](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CHxhjDPKfbY) — a canonical David Allen talk.

## Books

### Science

- [Feynman, R. P., and Leighton, R. (1985). *Surely You’re Joking, Mr. Feynman!*](https://books.google.com/books?vid=ISBN9780393316048) — lively stories from a great scientific mind.
- [James Gleick, *Chaos: Making a New Science*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/321477/chaos-by-james-gleick/) — a great popular introduction to chaos theory.
- [James Gleick, *The Information: A History, a Theory, a Flood*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/60765/the-information-by-james-gleick/) — a broad history of information as a scientific and cultural idea.
- [Carly Anne York, *The Salmon Cannon and the Levitating Frog*](https://www.basicbooks.com/titles/carly-anne-york/the-salmon-cannon-and-the-levitating-frog/9781541605213/) — curious, funny, and serious science writing about the odd paths discovery can take.
